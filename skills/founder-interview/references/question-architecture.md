# Question architecture — the ground the interview must cover

This is the field map the interview fills, the kind of question that fills each field to
quality, and a **calibration exemplar** of the depth the concierge build reached. The
exemplar is the *bar for depth and warmth* — **not** a template. Adapt every question to
*this* founder's vertical and vocabulary. Never paste one niche's specifics onto another.

**How to read the exemplar column:** the concierge build (a garden-design coach, "John")
is quoted only to show you what "deep enough" sounds like — pains in the customer's own
words, a *named* method, a real pricing decision, a concrete transformation. When you
interview a SaaS founder, a fitness coach, or a fractional CFO, you are chasing that same
*depth*, in *their* world. If your captured field is thinner than the exemplar, you're not
done.

---

## Domain 1 — Avatar / ICP

**Fills:** `ideal_customer`, `transformation`, `perceived_problem`, `root_cause`,
`daily_pain_points`, `secret_desires`, `common_mistakes`, `limiting_beliefs`,
`empowering_truths`.

**Ask for the lived moment, not the demographic.** You want the sentence you could put on
the page in quotes.

- "Picture the person this is for the moment right before they find you — where are they,
  what just happened, what are they feeling? Don't give me an age bracket; give me the
  Tuesday."
- "What do they tell themselves about why they haven't fixed this yet?" → `limiting_beliefs`
- "What have they already tried that didn't work — and what did that cost them?" →
  `common_mistakes`, `perceived_problem`
- "If this works, what's actually different in their life six months out — not the metric,
  the feeling?" → `transformation`, `secret_desires`
- "What's the thing they'd never say out loud but is the real reason they want this?" →
  `secret_desires`
- "What's true about them that they don't believe yet — the thing you'd tell them to their
  face?" → `empowering_truths`

**Depth bar (exemplar):** the concierge avatar wasn't "homeowners 45–65." It was a person
who *"parked in her own driveway, walked past her own backyard for years, and felt a small
tired guilt every time"* — the perceived problem ("my yard is a chore / an eyesore"), the
root cause ("I don't think I'm a designer, so I never start"), and the secret desire ("a
place that feels like mine, that I'm a little proud of"). That's the resolution you want.

## Domain 2 — Founder story & credibility

**Fills:** `struggle_story`, `breakthrough_moment`, `credibility_experience`,
`signature_method`.

- "How did you end up doing this — what were *you* struggling with before you cracked it?"
  → `struggle_story`
- "Was there a moment it clicked — where the thing you now teach first worked?" →
  `breakthrough_moment`
- "Why should someone trust you specifically with this? Years, results, credentials,
  scars — whatever's real." → `credibility_experience`
- "Do you have a name for how you do it — a method, a framework, a process? If not, walk me
  through your steps and let's name it together." → `signature_method`

**Depth bar (exemplar):** "30+ years" of real experience **plus a named, ownable method** —
the concierge build had the *"Garden Sanctuary Design Process"* (a GARDEN acronym). A named
method converts because it makes the transformation feel like a *system*, not the founder's
luck. If the founder has no name yet, help them name their real steps — do not invent
credentials they don't have.

## Domain 3 — Belief shifts

**Fills:** `vehicle_belief_shift`, `internal_belief_shift`, `external_belief_shift`,
`belief_shift_story`.

The webinar exists to move *one core belief*. Find the three shifts:

- **External** (about the world/approach): "What does your person believe about how this is
  *supposed* to be done that's actually wrong?" — exemplar: *big expensive blitz → one
  gentle step at a time.*
- **Internal** (about themselves): "What do they believe about *themselves* that stops
  them?" — exemplar: *"I'm not a designer" → the process does the seeing for you.*
- **Vehicle** (about your offer/method): "Why will *your* way work when what they tried
  didn't?"
- The **one** shift the webinar must land — name it explicitly. It becomes the memo's
  "the one belief this must shift."

## Domain 4 — The offer

**Fills:** `offer_name`, `pricing` (JSONB: regular / webinar), `guarantee`, `bonuses`,
`deliverables`, `delivery_process`, offer mechanism + differentiation + selling narrative
(persist to the offer/proof intake fields the deep-capture write path exposes).

- "What exactly do they get — walk me through the deliverables like I'm about to buy." →
  `deliverables`, `delivery_process`
- "What's it called, and what does it cost?" → `offer_name`, `pricing`
- "What's your guarantee? A real one lowers the risk of saying yes." → `guarantee`
- "What makes your version different from the three other things they could buy instead?"
  → differentiation
- "In one breath: why does your mechanism actually work?" → mechanism explanation

**Depth bar (exemplar):** the concierge offer wasn't just "a course for $X." It carried a
real **pricing decision** — anchored high at $2,999, then sold a **$399 founding-cohort
entry** (credited toward the full program), with a **14-day guarantee** and a stacked set
of bonuses. The *reason* for that structure (cold traffic couldn't clear a $2,999 front
end — see the strategist playbook) is exactly the kind of thinking you bring, not just
record.

## Domain 5 — Proof

**Fills:** `proof_points`, structured testimonials, `credentials`, `impact_metrics`, and a
market-fit signal.

- "Tell me about one client — where were they before, what did you do, where are they now?"
  → one concrete transformation (the single highest-leverage asset for a premium webinar)
- "Any numbers you can stand behind honestly — results, counts, revenue, ratings?" →
  `impact_metrics`
- "Have people said yes to this before, even informally? What did they say?" →
  market-fit signal
- If there's genuinely no proof yet: capture that **honestly** as a "no proof yet" flag —
  never fabricate one. (See strategist playbook: thin-proof challenge.)

---

## Domain 6 — Objections & CTA

**Fills:** `top_objections` (JSONB array), `call_to_action`.

- "When someone *doesn't* buy, what's the reason — the real one?" → `top_objections`
- Press for the top three. Most offers share a spine: **"is it worth it / can I afford
  it"**, **"will it work for *my* situation"**, **"I don't have time."**
- "What's the one action you want them to take at the end?" → `call_to_action`

**Depth bar (exemplar):** the three objections were answered structurally, not with
pressure — worth-it/afford-it by the value-anchor + founding price + guarantee;
will-it-work-for-me by the process being beginner-safe; no-time by the gentle one-step-at-
a-time pace. Capture the objection *and* the honest answer.

## Domain 7 — Traffic reality

**Fills:** existing audience + primary channel (persist to the deep-capture intake).

- "Who can you actually reach today — a list, a following, a referral stream, or are we
  starting cold?" → existing audience
- "Where will the first hundred registrations come from — ads, email, organic, partners?"
  → primary channel

**Why this matters (do not skip it):** traffic temperature drives the pricing and message
strategy. The exemplar's whole pricing re-design came from one fact — the plan was **cold
ads at ~$10/day, not a warm list**. Cold traffic can't clear a high front-end price. If you
skip traffic, you can't run the price-vs-traffic challenge, and you'll build a funnel priced
for an audience the founder doesn't have.

## Domain 8 — Voice / sender

**Fills:** brand voice (the `hasBrandVoiceOnRecord` gate must pass), `sender_name`,
`sender_email`, plus the persisted **voice card** (see `voice-card.md`).

- Capture voice **by listening** across the whole interview, not with a "describe your
  brand voice" question. Notice their verbs, metaphors, and the words they'd never use.
- Confirm the human sender: "Who are these emails coming from — your name, your email?"

**Depth bar (exemplar):** the concierge voice was **anti-hustle, plain-spoken, gentle** —
lowercase subject lines, no hard dollar figures in body copy, no urgency theater. That voice
was a *rule set* the whole funnel obeyed. Yours will be different for a different founder —
capture *their* actual voice, with a real ban list.
